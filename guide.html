User Guide

Learn how to use the Job Automation Risk Calculator and understand your results

Getting Started

This job automation risk calculator helps you understand how vulnerable your role is to AI automation. Follow these steps to get the most accurate assessment:

Step 1: Select Your Role Category

Start by selecting your role category from the predefined options. This will pre-fill many of the assessment criteria with typical values for your profession, including:

  • Software/Tech roles
  • Administrative positions
  • Customer Service
  • Data Analysis
  • Finance/Accounting
  • Sales/Marketing
  • Creative/Design

Step 2: Review and Customize

After selecting a role, carefully review each assessment section. The calculator uses several key factors to determine automation risk.

Understanding the Assessment Factors

Task Characteristics

This section evaluates the nature of your daily work. Consider:

  • Routine vs. Varied: How repetitive are your tasks?
  • Digital vs. Physical: Can your work be done entirely on a computer?
  • Rule-Based vs. Judgment: Do you follow clear procedures or make complex decisions?
  • Data Processing: How much of your work involves structured data?

Work Environment

Your workplace context significantly affects automation potential:

  • Industry Tech Adoption: How quickly does your industry adopt new technologies?
  • Company Size: Larger organizations often have more resources for automation
  • Regulatory Constraints: Some industries have strict regulations that slow automation
  • Remote Capability: Can your work be done remotely?

Economic Factors

Economic pressures influence automation decisions:

  • Labor Costs: Higher labor costs create more incentive to automate
  • ROI Timeline: How quickly would automation pay for itself?
  • Market Competition: Competitive industries may automate faster

Human Interaction Requirements

Some aspects of work are difficult to automate:

  • Empathy and Emotional Intelligence: Building trust and relationships
  • Creative Problem Solving: Novel situations requiring human judgment
  • Physical Presence: Tasks requiring in-person interaction
  • Collaboration: Complex teamwork and coordination

Interpreting Your Results

Risk Score Breakdown

The calculator provides a comprehensive risk score along with detailed breakdowns by category. Your overall score indicates:

  • High Risk (70-100%): Roles highly susceptible to automation in the near term
  • Medium Risk (40-69%): Roles that may see significant AI augmentation
  • Low Risk (0-39%): Roles requiring substantial human skills and judgment

Using Your Results

Use these insights to make informed decisions:

  • Identify areas where you might be most vulnerable
  • Plan skill development in areas that are harder to automate
  • Make informed career decisions and transitions
  • Understand which aspects of your role to emphasize or develop

Important Considerations

Model Limitations

Remember that this is a model-based prediction. Your actual risk may vary based on factors like:

  • Your specific employer's adoption rate of AI technologies
  • Your unique skills, experience, and professional network
  • Regulatory changes in your industry or region
  • The pace of AI development in your specific domain
  • Economic conditions and labor market dynamics
  • Unforeseen technological breakthroughs or limitations

Taking Action

After reviewing your results, consider these proactive steps to future-proof your career:

  • Develop complementary skills: Focus on skills that work alongside AI rather than compete with it
  • Build human-centric capabilities: Strengthen interpersonal, creative, and strategic thinking skills
  • Stay informed: Keep up with AI developments in your field
  • Network actively: Human relationships remain irreplaceable
  • Consider transitions: If your risk is high, explore adjacent roles with lower automation potential
