How this works

The stacked hazard job loss model

This publication distills a stacked hazard framework for thinking about automation risk. Tune the sliders above to explore how different progress assumptions bend the hazard curve—faster capability compounding or lower friction pulls the timeline forward.

Survival relationship
$$P_{\text{loss}}(t) = 1 - S(t) = 1 - \exp\left(-\int_0^t \lambda_{\text{total}}(s) \, ds\right)$$
Total hazard (stacked framework)
$$\lambda_{\text{total}}(s) = \lambda_{\text{AI}}(s) + \lambda_{\text{macro}}(s) + \lambda_{\text{firm}}(s) + \lambda_{\text{role}}(s) + \lambda_{\text{personal}}(s)$$

Right now the interactive experience concentrates on the AI hazard term. As you answer the prompts the curve shifts to reflect capability readiness, friction, and adaptability.

AI hazard component
$$\lambda_{\text{AI}}(s) = \frac{h_{\text{AI}}}{1 + \exp\{-\gamma [A(s) - \theta_0 - \kappa H(s) - \beta T_{\text{adapt}}(s)]\}}$$
  • s: Time (years)
  • λAI(s): AI hazard (annual chance of AI displacement)
  • A(s): AI readiness for your tasks (capability, reliability, cost)
  • H(s): Human/organizational friction
  • Tadapt(s): Task adaptability
  • θ0: Baseline adoption threshold
  • γ: Steepness of the adoption curve
  • κ: Sensitivity to friction
  • β: Sensitivity to adaptability
  • hAI: Hazard scaling factor
